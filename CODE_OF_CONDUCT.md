# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Violet's Revenge is built by students earning practicum credit, professionals rebuilding their careers, and volunteers who care about the project. Everyone here deserves a respectful, safe, and professional environment — regardless of experience level, background, or how long they've been on the team.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat all contributors with respect, on GitHub, Discord, and anywhere else the team communicates.
- Give and receive feedback constructively — critique the work, not the person.
- Support newer contributors and students; mentorship is part of what this project exists to provide.
- Credit others' work accurately and don't take credit for contributions that aren't yours.
- Respect the trauma-informed content guidelines in `docs/SENSITIVE-CONTENT-GUIDE.md` when discussing or creating story material.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Gatekeeping — dismissing contributors for their experience level, background, or how they arrived at the project.
- Sharing unreleased project material (builds, art, story content) outside approved channels.
- Deliberately undermining another contributor's work, credit, or standing on the team.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces: GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions), Discord, and any other official team communication channel, as well as any externship/practicum interactions conducted on behalf of the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code:

1. Report it to a project lead directly (Discord DM or email, not a public channel).
2. Include what happened, when, and any relevant context (screenshots, links).
3. Reports are handled confidentially, with information shared only as needed to address the issue.

Externship students: conduct issues do not automatically affect practicum sign-off or documented hours unless the underlying conduct itself is the issue.

## Enforcement

Project leads will review reports and take action proportional to the situation — this may range from a private conversation to removal from the project. Repeated or severe violations will result in removal from all project spaces.

## Acknowledgment

By contributing to this project, you agree to uphold this Code of Conduct.
