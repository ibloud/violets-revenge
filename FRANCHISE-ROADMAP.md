# 🩸 Violet's Revenge: Franchise Roadmap

A phased roadmap taking *Violet's Revenge* from Game Jam prototype to full asymmetrical horror franchise, within a *Dead by Daylight*-style framework — leaning into Gothic Horror, Psychological Thriller, and Supernatural Revenge. The Guilty are anonymized, masked entities (animal/pig masks); Violet is the supernatural Killer/Executioner hunting them.

---

## Phase 1: The Game Jam Prototype (MVP)
**Title:** *Violet's Revenge: The Morgue*
**Goal:** Prove the core gameplay loop and atmosphere in a small, contained environment.

- **Format:** 1v1 Asymmetrical Horror (one player is Violet, one player is the "Pig" Assailant trying to escape).
- **The Map:** A tight, claustrophobic Forensics Morgue and Office.
- **The Loop:**
  - **The Assailant** must search for Case Files hidden in desks and lockers, then take them to the Incinerator to destroy the evidence, which unlocks the exit door.
  - **Violet** must hunt down the Assailant. She cannot run fast, but she can set traps.
- **Violet's Mechanics:**
  - *The Nightingale:* Violet paints symbols on the walls (her unfinished painting). These act as sound traps — if the Assailant runs past them, the Nightingale sings, revealing their location.
  - *The Sachet:* Violet can sprinkle grave dirt (from her sachet) in doorways. If the Assailant steps in it, they become "heavy" and slowed for 3 seconds.
- **The Catch:** When Violet catches the Assailant, she doesn't kill them immediately — she drags them to the Autopsy Table (the "Hook" equivalent) to be judged.

---

## Phase 2: The Full Game (The Dead by Daylight Evolution)
**Title:** *Violet's Revenge: Hallows Eve*
**Goal:** Expand the 1v1 into a 1v4 franchise-ready multiplayer experience.

**The Lore Transition:** Violet didn't survive the physical attack; she died on that London street listening to sirens. But her rage, combined with the dark ritual she performed at Jenny's grave on Hallows Eve, tethered her spirit to the mortal plane. She now pulls abusers, enablers, and the corrupt into "The Purge" — a purgatory dimension where she makes them pay.

**The 1v4 Gameplay Loop:**
- **The Survivors (The Guilty):** Players take on the role of masked, anonymized culprits (The Pig, The Animal, etc.), dropped into a map and required to complete objectives to escape Violet's judgment.
- **The Objective:** Instead of repairing generators, the Guilty must "Sanitize Crime Scenes" — locate blood samples and case files and correctly process them in forensics machines. A mistake triggers an alarm, alerting Violet.
- **The Hooks (The Autopsy Slabs):** When Violet downs a Guilty player, she drags them to a Morgue Slab. They're strapped down and a supernatural autopsy begins. If not rescued by teammates in time, they're "cremated" and eliminated from the match.

**Violet's Killer Powers (Loadout):**
1. **The Nightingale's Canvas** — Violet places an invisible ward on a surface. When a Guilty player passes it, a piercing bird song echoes, granting Violet a visual aura of the player for 4 seconds.
2. **Grave Dirt Sachet** — Violet throws a pouch of dirt, creating a small field that mutes the Guilty player's footsteps and disables sprinting.
3. **Hockey Stick Strike (Weapon)** — A heavy, brutal melee weapon with a wide arc. Slower to swing, but on hit applies "Hemorrhage," causing the Guilty player to bleed faster and leave a trail.

## Phase 3: Building the Franchise

To expand into a franchise, the game needs replayability, diverse rosters, and deep lore.

### 1. The Vigilante Roster (New Killers)
Violet is just the first. The franchise can introduce other wronged souls from different eras and backgrounds, united by the theme of revenge against the unpunished:
- **The Intern** — a medical student whose complaints against a predatory superior were covered up.
- **The Arsonist** — a firefighter whose team was left to die by a corrupt captain.
- **The Orphan** — a grown child from Violet's case files who aged out of the system and seeks revenge on the system that failed their mother.

### 2. The Map Roster (The Crime Scenes)
Maps grounded in reality but twisted by supernatural purgatory:
- **St. Mary's Morgue** — Violet's home turf. Cold steel, flickering fluorescent lights, buzzing freezers.
- **The London Alley** — the site of her death. Raining constantly, narrow corridors, echoing distant sirens.
- **The Hockey Arena** — represents her past (the #88 jersey). Ice surfaces cause players to slide, making chases unpredictable.

### 3. The Progression System (The Forensics Tree)
Players level up by completing "Case Studies" rather than unlocking generic perks:
- **For Violet (Killers):** Upgrading her forensic tools — e.g., upgrading *Luminol* so she can see the exact paths the Guilty walked for the first 10 seconds of a match.
- **For The Guilty (Survivors):** Unlocking "Alibis" — perks to temporarily mask scratch marks, sabotage Autopsy Slabs, or heal faster using stolen medical supplies.

### 4. Transmedia Lore Expansion (The Case Files)
Building community through lore outside the game:
- **Web Series/Comics** — short stories about the victims from Violet's case files (Who was Jenny? What happened to Screech?).
- **Interactive ARG** — real-world forensic puzzles hidden in the game's promotional website; solving them unlocks exclusive in-game cosmetics (e.g. a golden hockey stick, a variant Nightingale painting).

---

## Why This Works as a Franchise
*Dead by Daylight* thrives on iconic killers and a desperate survival loop. *Violet's Revenge* takes that formula but grounds it in a gritty, emotionally resonant theme: **justice for the voiceless.** Making the Survivors the villains flips the psychological script — players aren't just trying to survive, they feel the weight of being hunted by someone who rightfully wants them dead.
