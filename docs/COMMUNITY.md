# Community Guidelines

Violet's Revenge is built by a distributed team of externship students, laid-off professionals rebuilding their portfolios, and volunteer contributors. This document covers how we work together — day to day, across Discord, GitHub, and beyond.

## Where We Work

- **GitHub** — source of truth for code, issues, and pull requests. If it's not tracked here, it's not officially part of the project.
- **Discord** — day-to-day communication, playtesting coordination, and team culture. See `docs/PLAYTEST-GUIDELINES.md` for playtest-specific rules.
- **Jira / GitHub Projects** — sprint planning and task tracking for the AGILE workflow referenced in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## Who We Are

This is a grassroots, bootstrapped team operating on a revenue-share model (see the README's Financial Waterfall section) rather than upfront pay. Because of that, we lean heavily on:

- **Documented process** — practicum hours, externship sign-off, and rev-share points all depend on work being visible and attributable. Do your work in the open: PRs, commits, and issue comments, not side-channel DMs.
- **Mentorship over gatekeeping** — senior contributors are here to mentor junior/student contributors, not just review their work. If you're senior, take the time. If you're junior, ask questions early.
- **Mutual respect across experience levels** — everyone here is rebuilding something, whether that's a resume, a portfolio, or a graduation requirement. Treat that with the seriousness it deserves.

## Communication Norms

- **Default to public channels.** Questions asked in open channels help the next person with the same question. Save DMs for genuinely private matters (personal accommodations, conflict resolution, etc.).
- **Be direct, be kind.** Code review and creative feedback should be honest — this is meant to be real industry experience — but critique the work, not the person.
- **Assume good faith, but flag real problems.** If something crosses a line (see `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`), raise it with a team lead rather than letting it fester.
- **Content sensitivity.** Given the trauma-informed themes in this project (see `docs/SENSITIVE-CONTENT-GUIDE.md`), keep discussion of sensitive story elements considerate — both in how it's written and where it's posted.

## Contribution Culture

- **Claim before you build.** Check `#good-first-issues` or the GitHub Projects board and claim a task before starting work, so effort isn't duplicated.
- **Small PRs over big ones.** Easier to review, easier to credit accurately for rev-share points, easier to merge without conflict.
- **Document as you go.** If you build a system, mechanic, or asset pipeline, leave enough of a trail (comments, a short doc, a PR description) that someone else on the team could pick it up.
- **Lore and character changes route through the Bible.** Nothing is canon until it's in `docs/STORY-BIBLE.md` or `docs/CHARACTER-BIBLE.md`. Propose changes there before building around them.

## Conflict & Escalation

- Most disagreements (creative direction, code approach, scope) should get resolved in the relevant PR or issue thread.
- Anything involving conduct, harassment, or a violation of `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` goes to a team lead directly, not a public channel.
- Disputes over rev-share point valuation or vesting are handled per the process in `docs/EXTERNSHIP-AGREEMENT.md`.

## Leaving the Team

Contributors are free to step away at any point. Per the README's vesting terms, your historical point contributions are honored for 12 months post-launch if you leave before Phase 2, or for the life of the game if you stay through launch as a Founding Developer. Let a team lead know so your transition (and any handoff of in-progress work) is documented.
